Catalog number: 39382
Launch Date: November 20, 2013 Status: Operational.

Downlink: 145.825 MHz and 437.325 MHz FM FSK beacon will be in AX.25 and CW
Uplink:     145.825 MHz and 437.325 MHz FM FSK beacon will be in AX.25 and CW
Callsign:    W5UL

CAPE II operates on 145.825 MHz with a CW beacon with the callsign W5UL, it also includes a digipeater, text to speech operation, a simplex repeater, email and tweet functions. The ground station software can be downloaded from http://www.ulcape.org

Frequency: 145.825 (varies due to Doppler shift)

Modulation: FM/CW

Beacon Interval: Varies Based on Modes (alternating ax25 & CW)

Interval: Green: 2 Min. Orange: 4 Min. Red: 6 Min.

CW Format of Data: Callsign, Satellite Name, Mode of Satellite (2 Character designation), Unregulated Bus Voltage

ax25 Format of Data: Callsign, Satellite Name, Beacon ID, Mode of Satellite, Current Satellite Time, Unregulated Bus Voltage, #Beacons since last reset, VHF Transmitter Temperature, UHF Transmitter Temperature.

CAPE is a completely nonprofit and student run organization which develops and builds Pico satellites along with a host of other aerospace and mission related systems such as weather balloons and sensor buoys.

Our facilities are provided by the University of Louisiana at Lafayette's Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering.

The goal of this project is to give students at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette the opportunity to research, design, develop, and maintain a low earth orbiting satellite.
