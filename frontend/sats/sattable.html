TabletSat-Aurora

Catalog number: 40017
Launch Date: June 19, 2014
Status: Operational

Downlinks: 437.050 MHz FM DSTAR and Parrot 800 mW to 2W
435.550 MHz and 436.100 MHz GMSK @ 4800 BPS
   8.192 GHz

TabletSat-Aurora is a Russian micro-satellite launched in 2014. The satellite is built in shape of hexagonal prism, with 6 foladable solar panels. It is claimed to be the first privately developed satellite in Russian Federation

TabletSat-Aurora was launched from Dombarovsky air base site 13, Russia, on 19 June 2014 by a Dnepr-1 rocket. Two-side communication with Earth was successfully established soon after launch.

Mission intended to verify technologies for the future satellite constellation detecting natural disasters. Main payload is the panchromatic photo camera capable of 15m resolution at nadir.

It carries a D-STAR Parrot (Store and Forward) Repeater running 0.8 watts of GMSK on 437.050 MHz (+/- 10 kHz) to a whip antenna. It can store up to 8 seconds of voice message.

There are two other transceivers that operate on 435.550 MHz and 436.100 MHz.

Their power can be varied by command from the ground station between 0.8 and 2.0 watts and it is understood they will be used for command and control and transmit GMSK telemetry data. There is also a downlink on 8192 MHz.

Dmitry Pashkov R4UAB reports the D-STAR repeater could become operational in early July.

When the D-STAR repeater is active the telemetry transmitters will be inactive.
