SWAYAN

Catalog number: 41607
Launched: June 22, 2016
Status: Operational

Beacon: 437.025 MHz, CW A1A    Dipole  Antenna 250mW
Downlink:  437.025 MHz, BFSK 1200 baud Shift 9600Hz  Error Correction
Uplink:  437.025 MHz, BFSK 1200 baud Shift 9600Hz  Error Correction

Swayam had been launched on the 22th of June in 2016 along with ISRO's CARTOSAT-2C by Polar Satellite Launch vehicle PSLV-C34.

Height of orbit is 500km with 97-98 deg inclination.
Swayam has a half duplex communication system operating at a frequency of 437.025 MHz.
It is a passively stabilized satellite with a dipole antenna.

Track Beacon:
Swayam's beacon broadcasts critical health parameters of the satellite.
It is an autonomous beacon of quarter watt power transmitted every 90 seconds in CW mode.
Beacon duration is expected to be between 6-10 seconds depending upon the transmission data.
It is encoded in morse code with rate being 15 words per minute.
Beacon frame starts with the call sign 'SWAYAM' followed by beacon data.
When the satellite is in low power mode the beacon is of shorter duration.
Anyone can listen to beacon using any commercial radio (e.g. Kenwood TS2000, ICOM etc.) which supports CW mode.

Digital Communication with Swayam:
The satellite is in reception state and transmits digital data only on request.
All digital packets of Swayam are encoded within a custom network layer protocol - COEP Satellite Protocol.
The modulation scheme used is binary frequency shift keying on the 437.025 MHz carrier.
The baud rate is 1200bps and the BFSK deviation is set to 9.6 kHz from the carrier frequency.
The satellites radiates 1 watt or 30 dBm power through its dipole antenna.
